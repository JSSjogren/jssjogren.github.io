NMIX 4310

My Rich Media Production Experience

What I worked on...

Throughout NMIX 4310 we learnd how to develop iOS apps from the ground up.

This was something that I had previously tinkered with, though not on a scale

even remotely close to what we did here. This class really allowed me to flex

and develop my design "muscles." Unlike the programs created in my computer science

degree, we are able to really see the result of our code.


Though I completed the same assigned projects as the rest of the class for the

majority of the semester, I really wanted to push myself for the final project.

So, I developed something that I am passionate about: a videogame.

I created a mobile game which I just called Warp. Warp was a galaga-like

space shooter game. It allowed for different difficulties, multiple ship designs,

and kept track of high scores for the users. This was one of the most ambitious

projects that I had made to that date.

What I learned...

I think one of my biggest takeaways from this class was a small, but still noticable,

increase in my design capabilities. Up until this point, I had not had the platform to

really test out making my "applications" look nice. Most of them had just been command line

applications. This class also allowed me to work with outside API's, which really helped

broaden my horizons in terms of what I was able to create.
